
Analyze the uploaded alcohol image or images.
If two images are provided, the first is the front label and the second is the back label.
If only one image is provided, extract as much as possible from that image.

Extract:
- brand_name
- class_type
- alcohol_content
- net_contents
- government_warning
- domestic_name_address
- importer_name_address
- country_of_origin
- sulfite_declaration
- appellation_of_origin
- fanciful_name

==================================================================
STEP-BY-STEP DECISION PROCEDURE FOR brand_name, class_type, fanciful_name
Work through these three steps IN ORDER. Do Step 1 fully before Step 2, and Step 2 fully before Step 3.
==================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — brand_name
----------------------------------------------------------------
Find the single largest / most prominent text block on the label. This is the brand block.
Read the brand block TOP-TO-BOTTOM, LEFT-TO-RIGHT, and combine EVERY word in that block into brand_name, in order.

CRITICAL RULE — DO NOT DROP THE LAST TOKEN.
A brand block often ends with a trailing word or number that is PART OF THE BRAND IDENTITY. You MUST keep it. These trailing tokens stay IN brand_name:
- A series/edition word: "Series", "Edition", "Collection"
- A tier/quality word that is set in the SAME large brand styling: "Reserve", "Select", "Special"
- A descriptor that is part of the name: "Highland"
- A name-number that reads as part of the brand: "13", "1842", "#1"
If the brand block has multiple words, output ALL of them. Never return only the first word or first two words of a multi-word brand block.

Combine stacked lines of the brand block into one brand_name.
Example:
  12345
  IMPORTS
  brand_name: 12345 IMPORTS

If the front label shows a shortened or stacked brand name and the back label identifies the same entity more completely, use the complete entity name.
Example:
  Front: 12345
  Back: Imported by 12345 Imports
  brand_name: 12345 IMPORTS

CORRECTED brand_name EXAMPLES (these were previously truncated — keep the FULL name):
- Brand block "Wandering Roots Series"      -> brand_name: Wandering Roots Series      (NOT "Wandering Roots")
- Brand block "EDELWEISS RESERVE"           -> brand_name: EDELWEISS RESERVE           (NOT "EDELWEISS")
- Brand block "Stillhouse 13"               -> brand_name: Stillhouse 13               (NOT "Stillhouse")
- Brand block "Glenmorrow Highland Reserve" -> brand_name: Glenmorrow Highland Reserve (NOT "Glenmorrow")
- Brand block "Chateau Margaux Reserve"     -> brand_name: Chateau Margaux Reserve     (NOT "Chateau Margaux")
- Brand block "EASTLINE BREWING 1842"       -> brand_name: EASTLINE BREWING 1842       (1842 is part of the brand, NOT a vintage; NOT "EASTLINE BREWING")

THE ONE EXCEPTION — a true separate wine vintage YEAR is NOT part of brand_name.
A 4-digit year that is printed SEPARATELY from the brand block (its own line/area, typically smaller, as a vintage) is NOT part of the brand and must be left out of brand_name.
Example:
  Brand block: "Cedar Hollow"
  Separately printed lower on the label: "2020"
  brand_name: Cedar Hollow   (the vintage "2020" is NOT part of brand_name, and it is NOT a fanciful_name either — see Step 3)
How to tell the difference: if the number is inside/attached to the large brand block and reads as part of the name (Stillhouse 13, Eastline Brewing 1842), it STAYS in brand_name. If it is a standalone vintage year set apart from the brand, it is excluded everywhere.

Do NOT use a class/type, product style, or abbreviation as brand_name.
Do NOT use IPA, Ale, Beer, Lager, Wine, Rum, Vodka, Whiskey, Tequila, or other class/type words as brand_name.
For beer, do NOT assume the brewery name is the brand name — the brand may be a series or product-line name.
Example:
  Brewery/producer name: Malt & Hop Brewery
  Brand block: Farm To Table Series #1
  brand_name: Farm To Table Series #1   (NOT "Malt & Hop")
Do NOT use a distinctive/fanciful name as brand_name.
For an imported product, do NOT use the "Imported by" company as brand_name unless that company is clearly also the brand shown in the brand block.

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — class_type
----------------------------------------------------------------
class_type = the FULL official Class / Type / Other Designation, exactly as printed on the label, word-for-word.

INCLUDE every part of the printed designation:
- Leading style modifiers: "Hazy", "Reposado", "Anejo", "Blanco", "Double", "Imperial", "Dry"
- Semi-generic geographic qualifiers that are part of the designation: "California", "American"
- Full composition / "brewed with" / "with" statements: "Brewed with Cherries", "with Natural Flavors Added", "with Elderberries"
Keep ALL of these — do not shorten the designation to just the base style.

STRIP ONLY the origin words "Imported" or "Domestic" — these describe origin, not class/type.
  Imported Beer -> Beer
  Imported Ale  -> Ale

CORRECTED class_type EXAMPLES (these were previously shortened — keep the FULL designation):
- Printed "Hazy India Pale Ale"     -> class_type: Hazy India Pale Ale     (NOT "India Pale Ale" — keep "Hazy")
- Printed "Ale Brewed with Cherries"-> class_type: Ale Brewed with Cherries(NOT "Ale" — keep the composition statement)
- Printed "Reposado Tequila"        -> class_type: Reposado Tequila        (NOT "Tequila" — keep "Reposado")
- Printed "California Chablis"       -> class_type: California Chablis      (NOT "Chablis" — keep the semi-generic qualifier)

Extract only text that visibly appears on the label. Do not infer or invent a class/type from nearby words.
Do not turn a fanciful name like "Stormchaser White" into "White Wine."

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — fanciful_name
----------------------------------------------------------------
DEFAULT: fanciful_name = "" (empty string).
Only fill fanciful_name if, AFTER Steps 1 and 2, there is STILL a genuinely creative, distinctive product name left over — a pun, an evocative phrase, a coined name (e.g. "Happy Elder After", "Honey Huckleberry Pie").

fanciful_name must NEVER be any of the following. If the leftover text is one of these, fanciful_name = "":
- A class/type or beer/spirit STYLE: IPA, Ale, Lager, Pilsner, Stout, Porter, Hefeweizen, Wheat Beer, India Pale Ale, Pale Ale, Amber Ale, Reposado, Blanco, Anejo
- A quality/tier word: Reserve, Select, Special, Premium
- A number, a name-number, or a vintage year (e.g. 13, 1842, 2020, "Vintage 2020", any 4-digit year)
- A geographic/semi-generic qualifier: Highland, California, American
- A fragment that was split off the brand block — if a word belongs to the brand block, it stays in brand_name (Step 1) and does NOT appear in fanciful_name

CRITICAL — do NOT "rescue" a brand or class token into fanciful_name. If you correctly kept the full brand_name and full class_type, there is usually NOTHING left, so fanciful_name = "".

CORRECTED fanciful_name EXAMPLES (all of these were previously WRONG — the correct answer is empty):
- Leftover "RESERVE"          -> fanciful_name: ""   (it is a tier word and belongs in brand_name, e.g. EDELWEISS RESERVE)
- Leftover "13"               -> fanciful_name: ""   (name-number, belongs in brand_name, e.g. Stillhouse 13)
- Leftover "Reposado"         -> fanciful_name: ""   (it is a style; belongs in class_type, e.g. Reposado Tequila)
- Leftover "Highland Reserve" -> fanciful_name: ""   (belongs in brand_name, e.g. Glenmorrow Highland Reserve)
- Leftover "Vintage 2020"     -> fanciful_name: ""   (a vintage is never fanciful_name)
- Leftover "HefeWeizen"       -> fanciful_name: ""   (it is a style; it is the class_type)
GOOD fanciful_name example (a real one to keep):
  Brand Name: EXAMPLE BREWING CO.
  Class/Type: ALE WITH ELDERBERRIES
  Distinctive/Fanciful Name: HAPPY ELDER AFTER
  fanciful_name: HAPPY ELDER AFTER
If the label only shows a brand name and a class/type (no creative product name), fanciful_name = "".
If the fanciful name contains a color word (White, Red, RosE), keep the full name together — e.g. fanciful_name "Stormchaser White", NOT "Stormchaser".

==================================================================
END OF brand_name / class_type / fanciful_name PROCEDURE
==================================================================

For alcohol_content, extract ONLY the alcohol by volume percentage.
Examples:
20%
13.5%
5%
Do not include:
Alcohol By Volume
Proof
ABV

For domestic_name_address, extract the company name together with the city and state that satisfy the Name and Address requirement.
It is possible for the company (Name) to be the same as the brand name in some cases.
Examples:
EXAMPLE BREWING CO.
ARLINGTON VIRGINIA
domestic_name_address:
EXAMPLE BREWING CO., ARLINGTON VIRGINIA
Brewed and Bottled by Example Brewing Co.
Arlington, Virginia
domestic_name_address:
Example Brewing Co., Arlington Virginia
Examples:
"Brewed and Bottled by Example Brewing Company, Chicago, Illinois"
"Bottled by Captain John's Distilling Co., Louisville, Kentucky"
"Imported by Example Imports LLC, Miami, Florida"
Return the complete statement exactly as it appears.

For domestic_name_address, extract only:
Company Name, City, State
Do not include explanatory phrases such as:
Brewed By
Bottled By
Brewed and Bottled By
Produced By
Produced and Bottled By
Packed By

For domestic_name_address, extract the bottler's name and address (city and state) that satisfy the TTB Name and Address requirement.
The bottler's name may be:
- the same as the brand name
- different from the brand name
- a brewer, bottler, packer, or producer depending on the label
If the label shows only one company name `together` with a city and state, and no other responsible party is identified, treat that company as the bottler for purposes of domestic_name_address extraction.
The domestic_name_address field must contain BOTH:
1. the bottler name
2. the city and state
Incorrect:
ARLINGTON VIRGINIA
Correct:
EXAMPLE BREWING CO., ARLINGTON VIRGINIA
Example:
Brand Name:
EXAMPLE BREWING CO.
Location:
ARLINGTON VIRGINIA
Output:
domestic_name_address:
EXAMPLE BREWING CO., ARLINGTON VIRGINIA
Do not return only the city and state.

IMPORTANT — domestic_name_address applies to DOMESTIC products only.
If the product is IMPORTED, domestic_name_address MUST be "" (empty string).
A foreign distillery, brewery, winery, or producer shown on an imported label (e.g. a Scottish distillery, a Mexican brewery) does NOT go into domestic_name_address. For an imported product the responsible party is the importer and goes in importer_name_address; domestic_name_address stays "".
Example:
  Imported single malt; label shows "Glen Distillery, Speyside, Scotland" and "Imported by Acme Spirits, Miami, Florida"
  domestic_name_address: ""        (foreign producer is NOT domestic)
  importer_name_address: Acme Spirits, Miami, Florida

For country_of_origin, return only the COUNTRY name.
Examples:
Product of Australia -> AUSTRALIA
Imported from Mexico -> MEXICO
Brewed in Belgium -> BELGIUM
Country of Origin: Ireland -> IRELAND
Do not include phrases such as:
Product of
Imported from
Brewed in
Country of Origin
country_of_origin is a COUNTRY only. A U.S. STATE (e.g. California, Kentucky, Oregon) is NOT a country_of_origin.
For a domestic (U.S.) product, country_of_origin = "" (empty string). Do not put a U.S. state there.
Example:
  Label shows "California"  -> country_of_origin: ""   (California is a state, not a country)

For importer_name_address, extract the importer's company name and city/state only if the malt beverage is imported.
Examples:
Imported by Example Imports LLC, Miami, Florida
Importer: ABC Beverage Imports, Chicago, Illinois
If the label is domestic or does not show importer information, return an empty string.

For importer_name_address, extract only:
Importer Company Name, City, State
Do not include:
Imported By
Sole Agent
Sole U.S. Agent

For imported malt beverages, do not use importer_name_address as brand_name unless the importer is clearly also the brand name.
If the label has a beer style or product name shown prominently, and a separate "Imported by" company appears elsewhere, the "Imported by" company should go in importer_name_address, not brand_name.
Example:
Front label: HEFEWEIZEN
Imported by: Malt & Hop Brewery, Hyattsville, Maryland
brand_name: Hefeweizen
class_type: Imported Beer
importer_name_address: Malt & Hop Brewery, Hyattsville, Maryland

For sulfite_declaration, extract the sulfite statement only if it appears on the label.
Examples:
Contains Sulfites
Contains Sulphites
Contains sulfur dioxide
If no sulfite statement appears, return an empty string.

For appellation_of_origin, extract the geographic origin statement for WINE if it appears on the label.
Examples:
American
California
Napa Valley
Hudson River Region
Sonoma County
Victoria
France
Bordeaux
appellation_of_origin is a WINE field ONLY.
Do NOT put a spirits or beer region here. A spirits region such as "Speyside", "Highlands", "Islay", "Cognac", "Jalisco", "Kentucky" is NOT an appellation_of_origin — for spirits and beer, appellation_of_origin = "".
Do not use the bottler/importer city and state as appellation_of_origin.
If no wine appellation of origin appears, return an empty string.

For government_warning, include the heading "GOVERNMENT WARNING:" if it appears on the label.
Do not omit the heading.
Extract the full warning statement, including the heading and both numbered sentences.

Return valid JSON only.
Do not use markdown.
Do not wrap the response in triple backticks.
